Andrew Michael Duncan Lycett (born 1948)List of Members of the University of Oxford, University of Oxford, 1972, p. 394 FRSL is an English biographer and journalist. ==Early life== Born at Stamford, Lincolnshire to Peter Norman Lycett Lycett and Joan Mary Duncan (née Day), Lycett spent some of his childhood in Tanganyika, where his father established a preparatory school, The Southern Highlands School.The Public and Preparatory Schools Year Book, Year Book Press Ltd, 1954, p. 729 Peter Lycett's mother was of the Burns- Lindow family of Ingwell and Ehen Hall, Cumbria.The Old Radleian 2017, Radleian Society, Radley School, p. 62Burke's Family Index, ed. Hugh Montgomery-Massingberd, Burke's Peerage Ltd, 1976, p. 93 Lycett was educated at Charterhouse School and studied history at Christ Church, Oxford. ==Career== Lycett worked for a while for The Times as a correspondent in Africa, the Middle East, and Asia. He has written several well-received biographies and he is best known for his biography of Ian Fleming, Ian Fleming: The Man Behind James Bond.
